THE 5 STAGES OF CHANGE — Where Are You?
The Transtheoretical Model explains how people actually break habits. You can't skip stages — but you can move through them faster.
1
PRE-CONTEMPLATION — "I don't have a problem"
You're not yet aware of the issue or are minimizing it. This is where most men spend years. The fact that you're here means you may have already passed this stage.
2
CONTEMPLATION — "Maybe I have a problem"
You see both sides — the benefits and the costs of the habit. You're ambivalent. This stage can last years. The key move is to tip the scales — write out every way the habit is costing you.
3
PREPARATION — "I'm going to change"
You've decided to change and you're getting ready. This is where you make the plan — remove the triggers, build the support, tell someone. Preparation done poorly leads to relapse.
4
ACTION — "I'm doing it"
You've made the change and you're living it. This requires the most energy. The first 30–90 days are the highest-risk period for relapse. Support, structure, and identity are critical here.
5
MAINTENANCE — "This is who I am now"
You've sustained the change for 6+ months. The habit no longer dominates your thoughts. This doesn't mean the risk is gone — it means you've built a new identity and new systems. Relapse is still possible but less likely.
⚠️ IF YOU'RE STRUGGLING WITH DEPENDENCE
This tool is for self-reflection and education — not treatment. If you're dealing with a serious substance dependence, please reach out for professional support. In Canada and the US, you can call 1-800-662-4357 (SAMHSA) or text "HELLO" to 741741 for the Crisis Text Line. You can also visit our Resources page for more help lines and support options. Asking for help is not weakness — it's the hardest and most important move you can make.
